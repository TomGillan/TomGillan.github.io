Designed as a response to the istd 'Lost' brief, this book is a critique of the Japanese working practice of over-work, and how it can lead to negative health both physically and mentally and in some cases death, which is known as Karoshi.

My aim was to produce a book that tells the story of karoshi to a western audience, using Japan as a warning of the current trend. The cover is representative of how from the outside the hardworking culture is presumed as the ideal, and it is only through closer attention, and through reading through the book, that the unsettling truth is revealed through the words of karoshi victims.

The books content is split between informative and emotional content. The informative content is made up of journalistic information and imagery, academic writings on the topic and how to prevent karoshi. To give a more human emotional aspect to the book personal stories and text produced by victims of karoshi where integrated throughout the book.

Both informative and emotional stories are presented to the reader at the same time, quotes in red on the transparent paper, and the informative content in black and white on off white paper.

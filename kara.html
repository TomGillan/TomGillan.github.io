Kara healthcare is a design concept completed for my degree show. The project speculates that advancements in health monitoring monitoring technology could allow for wide spread use of mobile devices to accurately monitor and provide care advice to patients living with long term health conditions.

The challenge was to design a whole new way of interacting with this technology so that people could live fuller happier lives, without having to deal with detailed data.

Kara is an AI character that represents a patients health and provides care advice dependant on they condition. The project required research into how people percieve and interact with AI. Kara’s design was kept simple and minimal taking inspiration from Japanese Kuwaii cuteness culture to ensure a friendly and caring appearance.

User personas representative of general users where used to represent typical people interviewed to determine how monitoring could help with their health conditions and help shape the design of the product as well as the visual identity.

These took the form of John, a 58 year old heart patient, Sarah a fourteen year old with diabeties, and Paul, who suffers from migraine.

Kara processes your data in real-time and provides gentle care suggestions throughout the day through the form of pop up notifications. The experience is kept as simple and effortless, with most interactions comping through the Kara character.

For more confident users, there are side screens where they can review what data Kara is monitoring, check their care plan, as well as edit their own medical Profile. In the event of an emergency Kara displays your emergency details and can alert emergency services.
